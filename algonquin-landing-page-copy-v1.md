### [ACCOMMODATION 1 — Four Corners Algonquin]

**Badge:** New for 2026

**H3:** Four Corners Algonquin — Glamping

**Body:**
This is the new one. Four Corners is a luxury glamping site on the east end of the park, near the town of Whitney. You get a dome or a cabin, a real bed, and access to the old rail trail that cuts through Algonquin. Whitney has outfitters if you want to add a paddle or a hike.

We drop you Friday by noon. Their shuttle covers the Highway 60 corridor if you want to explore further. We pick you up Sunday at 4:30 PM.

Book your accommodation directly on the [Four Corners website](https://www.fourcornersalgonquin.ca/) — do it early, they fill up.

---

### [ACCOMMODATION 2 — Wolf Den Bunkhouse]

**H3:** Wolf Den Bunkhouse — Budget-friendly

**Body:**
The Wolf Den has been the go-to for Parkbus weekenders for years. Cozy cabins, private and semi-private rooms, communal vibe. It's technically 6 km from the park gate, but the setting feels like you're already in it — hiking trails and Oxtongue Lake are walking distance.

Call to book: (705) 635-9336. Book early. They're popular for a reason.

---

### [ACCOMMODATION 3 — Campgrounds]

**H3:** Campgrounds inside the park

**Body:**
If you're bringing a tent, you have options along the Highway 60 corridor: Lake of Two Rivers (biggest, busiest), Mew Lake, and Pog Lake (quieter, more private). All are connected by the Rail Trail, so you can bike between them.

Book campsites through [Ontario Parks](https://reservations.ontarioparks.com/) well in advance — summer weekends sell out.

**[Small note:]** Other roofed options within walking distance of our stops: Algonquin Lakeside Inn, Blue Spruce Inn, Blue Spruce Resort. Inside the park, Killarney Lodge, Arowhon Pines Resort, and Bartlett Lodge are high-end options that can arrange shuttle service to our Lake of Two Rivers or Canoe Lake stops.

### Stop 1 — Oxtongue Lake
**Arrive:** 11:00 AM · **Depart:** 4:40 PM (day trips)

The first stop and the most popular for paddlers. Algonquin Outfitters' base is here — rent a canoe for a self-guided paddle to Ragged Falls (one of the area's biggest waterfalls) or book a guided North Canoe trip. The lake is calm and sheltered, good for all levels. Short hike at Beetle Lake trail, right from the drop-off. Small café on site.

**Good for:** Canoeing, kayaking, short hikes, waterfall access

---

### Stop 2 — Wolf Den Bunkhouse
**Arrive:** 11:05 AM · **Depart:** 4:30 PM (day trips)

Primarily a weekend stop — this is where you get off if you're staying at the Wolf Den. Ten-minute walk to Oxtongue Lake and Algonquin Outfitters.

**Good for:** Weekend accommodation, access to Oxtongue Lake activities

---

### Stop 3 — Canoe Lake (Portage Store)
**Arrive:** 11:25 AM · **Depart:** 4:25 PM (day trips)

The main activity hub on Highway 60. Algonquin Outfitters' Portage Store has a licensed sit-down restaurant, guided North Canoe trips, self-guided rentals, and an Ontario Parks permit office. This is also the launch point for backcountry canoe routes if you're heading deeper into the park for the weekend.

**Good for:** Lunch, guided paddles, starting backcountry trips, gear rentals

---

### Stop 4 — Lake of Two Rivers Store
**Arrive:** 11:35 AM · **Depart:** 4:10 PM (day trips)

The only stop with bike rentals. Grab a mountain bike and ride the old Rail Trail that connects the campgrounds. There's a restaurant and a small grocery store. The Highland Backpacking Trail starts a short walk from here.

**Good for:** Mountain biking, Rail Trail access, groceries, Highland Trail access

---

### Stop 5 — Pog Lake / Whitefish Campground
**Arrive:** 11:40 AM · **Depart:** 4:00 PM (day trips)

Quieter campground on the east side. Access point for Whitefish Group Campground. Connected to the other campgrounds by the Rail Trail. This stop operates until Labour Day only.

**Good for:** Camping (quieter alternative to Lake of Two Rivers), cycling

---

### Stop 6 — Four Corners Algonquin
**Arrive:** 12:00 PM · **Depart:** Sundays at 4:30 PM (weekend only — no same-day return)

**Badge:** New for 2026

The glamping stop. We drop you Friday, Four Corners' shuttle covers the rest. Pick-up is Sunday only.

**Good for:** Glamping weekends, access to Whitney and the Rail Trail

## [SECTION: GOOD TO KNOW]

**H2:** Before you go

[Design note: accordion/collapsible format. Keep these tight — this is the practical stuff people need, not the selling copy.]

---

**What's included in the ticket?**
Round-trip coach transportation and park admission. Gear rentals, food, and accommodation are separate.

**Can I bring my bike?**
Up to 3 regular bikes per bus (no e-bikes), first-come first-served. Email help@parkbus.ca in advance to reserve a spot. Folding bikes don't need pre-approval as long as they fit in a large-suitcase footprint.

**How much luggage can I bring?**
Suitcases, backpacks, coolers, camping gear — the bus has a full luggage compartment. No canoes, kayaks, fireworks, firewood, or firearms.

**Is there a bathroom on the bus?**
Yes, on most buses. There's also a 15-minute rest stop about halfway.

**Can I bring my dog?**
Yes. Pet ticket is $20 each way. Pets must be in a carrier or muzzled on the bus. Dogs must be leashed in the park. Owner is responsible for any cleaning fees.

**Is there cell service in the park?**
Spotty. You'll get signal on hilltops and some larger lakes, but don't count on it. Coverage varies by carrier.

**Where do I buy food in the park?**
Lake of Two Rivers Store has basic groceries and a fast-food restaurant (burgers, pizza, ice cream). The Portage Store at Canoe Lake has similar. Call ahead if you need something specific — options are limited.

**What about bears?**
They're in the park but generally avoid people. Store food properly. If you're camping at Lake of Two Rivers, Mew Lake, or Pog Lake, park staff provides free food lockers (key requires a $25 refundable cash deposit at the park office). See [Ontario's BearWise guide](https://www.ontario.ca/page/prevent-bear-encounters-bear-wise) for more.

**Can I request a custom stop?**
No — for safety and logistics reasons, we stick to our published stops. But we're always reviewing the route. Email us with suggestions: help@parkbus.ca.
