Pink granite, clear water, white pines

Killbear sits on a long peninsula jutting into the eastern shore of Georgian Bay, inside the UNESCO Georgian Bay Littoral Biosphere Reserve. The bedrock is pure Canadian Shield — two-billion-year-old granite, glacially scoured into smooth pink shelves that slope into water so clear you can watch fish shadowing your feet. White pines lean off the rock at impossible angles, shaped by decades of west wind off the 30,000 Islands.

The beach at Kilcoursie Bay is a 2 km horseshoe of sand — shallow, protected, and warm by mid-July. The Lookout Point Trail ends at a granite shelf with a wide-open view of Georgian Bay that makes people stop talking for a minute. And the Killbear Tree, the windswept pine at Sunset Rocks, is the reason the park ends up on postcards.

The park is about three hours north of Toronto, past Parry Sound. Day-tripping by car means six hours of driving for a few hours of beach. The Parkbus handles the 400, the 559, the parking, and the route through Parry Sound. You handle showing up with a towel and an appetite.
