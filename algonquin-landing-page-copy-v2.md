### Body

Algonquin is Ontario's oldest provincial park — bigger than Prince Edward Island, with over 2,400 lakes and some of the best paddling and cycling in the country. The Highway 60 corridor runs through the south end of the park, and that's where we take you. You choose your stop: paddle Oxtongue Lake, bike the old Rail Trail, eat lunch at a licensed restaurant on Canoe Lake, or just sit on a dock and do absolutely nothing.

Two ways to go: a day trip (out and back in one day, 5+ hours in the park) or a weekend trip (we drop you Friday, pick you up Sunday). Both include park admission. Both give you 15% off canoe and bike rentals from Algonquin Outfitters.

### Stop 1 — Oxtongue Lake

[Keep existing image: AOOxtongueB226.jpg]

**Estimated arrival:** 11:00 AM
**Departure:** 4:40 PM

The most popular stop for paddlers. Algonquin Outfitters' home base is right here — rent a canoe for a self-guided paddle on calm, sheltered water, or book a guided North Canoe trip to Ragged Falls, one of the area's biggest waterfalls. ## FAQ TAB

### Are there overnight options for non-campers?

Yes — and you have more options than ever.

**New for 2026:** [Four Corners Algonquin](https://www.fourcornersalgonquin.ca/) is a luxury glamping site on the east end of the park. Domes and cabins with real beds, near the town of Whitney. Book accommodations on their website in advance. We drop you Friday, their shuttle handles the rest, and we pick you up Sunday at 4:30 PM.

**Wolf Den Bunkhouse** — (705) 635-9336 — has been the Parkbus favourite for years. Cozy cabins, private and semi-private rooms. 6 km from the park gate, with hiking trails and canoe rentals within walking distance. Oxtongue Lake Algonquin Outfitters is about a 10-minute walk.

Wolf Den and Four Corners are the only locations where Parkbus currently stops. However, Algonquin Lakeside Inn, Blue Spruce Inn, and Blue Spruce Resort are all within walking distance of our stops. Inside the park, Killarney Lodge, Arowhon Pines Resort, and Bartlett Lodge are high-end options — they can typically arrange a shuttle to our Lake of Two Rivers Store or Portage Store stops.

---

### Can Parkbus offer extra stops on demand?

Unfortunately not — for safety, liability, and our agreements with the park and partners, we stick to our published stops. But we're always reviewing the route. If you have suggestions for future stops, get in touch at help@parkbus.ca.

---

### Are there places to buy food or camping equipment?

**Lake of Two Rivers Store** (one of our stops) has basic groceries — mostly non-perishables — and a fast-food restaurant with burgers, pizza, fries, and ice cream.

**Portage Store at Canoe Lake** (also one of our stops) offers similar products and services.

Call ahead if you need something specific. Options are limited and these are the only two spots along the corridor, so plan accordingly.

---

### Is there cell service in the park?

Spotty and unreliable — but improving. Best reception on hilltops and in the middle of larger lakes. Coverage depends heavily on your carrier: in the same spot, one phone might get LTE while another gets nothing. Plan as if you won't have service.

---

### Can I bring pets on the bus?

Yes. Pet ticket is $20 each way. Your pet must travel in a carrier or with a muzzle, and you're responsible for a cleaning fee if they make a mess. Dogs must be on leash in the park.

---

### Are there bears? How do I store food without a car?

Bears are in the park but generally avoid people — your chances of seeing one are extremely slim. See the [Ontario BearWise guide](https://www.ontario.ca/page/prevent-bear-encounters-bear-wise) for prevention tips.

You do need to store food properly. If you're camping at Lake of Two Rivers, Mew Lake, or Pog Lake, park staff provides free food lockers to Parkbus passengers. Lockers are near the comfort stations — get the key at the park office with a $25 refundable cash deposit.

---

### How much luggage can I bring? What about bikes?

We run 56-seater coaches with full luggage compartments. Suitcases, backpacks, coolers, and camping gear are all fine.

**Bikes:** Up to 3 regular bikes per bus (no e-bikes), first-come first-served. Email help@parkbus.ca in advance to reserve your spot. Folding bikes and folding/inflatable kayaks don't need pre-approval as long as they fit in a large-suitcase footprint.

**Not allowed:** Regular canoes, kayaks, fireworks, firewood, firearms.

---

### Is there a bathroom on the bus?

Yes, on most buses. We also make a 15-minute rest stop about halfway into the journey.
